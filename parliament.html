The only votes that are represented are the votes for the winning candidate of an amount equal to the runner up candidate plus one.

All other votes are wasted. Excess votes for winning candidates are also wasted. On an individual level it may be more appropriate to think of this waste as a fraction of a vote (e.g. if 20% of votes for a candidate were wasted, then each vote for that candidate was only worth 80% of a represented vote).

How many seats should our parliament have? Well I guess it's only fair if we take the number of seats in the House of Commons (HOC) and multiply that by the number of unrepresented votes divided by represented votes. In 2019, that was 14,367 votes per seat.

How should we assign seats? Proportional representation, of course, is the best way to reduce the number of unrepresented votes. Each party is given a share of seats proportional to its total vote share.

Who should sit in our parliament? The people the unrepresented voters voted for. In some cases, especially for the top Conservatives, they will also sit in the HOC. Members are ranked (within party) in order from most unrepresented votes to least.
